Objection 1 – Finances / Timing
\n
Status: Resolved – Closed on 6x$500

\n
“These goals are very, very serious. The issue is that financially, I'm still not like, this is still a very recent job to me… I'm still building up to see what a consistent check looks like… I need to build up the funds before I can fully commit here.”
\n
\n
\n Type\n Primary Money Objection (Ability) with element of Timing (“come back around March”)\n
\n
\n Root Cause\n Limited savings (~$2k total), new in role, uncertainty about paycheck consistency; not fear of results.\n
\n
\n
\n How Rep Handled It (Aligned with SOP):
\n • Matt first isolated alignment: confirmed Malo felt MW was the right fit (“you guys are a great team… goals could be met”).
\n • Asked SOP-style clarifying question: is it true lack of funds vs fear of not getting results (“Which one are you?”). Malo confirmed it was purely financial.
\n • Ben quantified available cash (“what do you have available today… cash on hand?”) to understand real ability.
\n • Matt explicitly avoided overburdening finances (“We never want to put fitness and your financial and your livelihood like at odds”).
\n • Offered a flexible structure: 6 x $500 with first payment now, next charge ~6 weeks out (one month after Jan 12 start), giving time to earn more in sales role.
\n • Framed flexibility as exception based on fit and story, preserving value perception.\n
\n
\n Coaching Opportunity:
\n • Earlier in discovery, ask the SOP “resourcefulness” question and monthly spend on fitness to pre-frame investment and reduce surprise at $3k.
\n • When offering 6x$500, explicitly restate that the total program value is still $3k and deliverables are unchanged, to avoid anchoring Malo to the lower monthly number as a “discounted” program.
